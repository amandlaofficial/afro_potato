Afro Maize Meal

The first step in this process is to dry clean the corn, separating fines and broken from the whole corn. Stones, dirt, metal and other foreign matter are also removed in the process. The maize is sortexed to remove any unsound grain. The clean corn is conditioned/tempered to 15.5 percent moisture. While moist, the majority of the outer bran or pericarp, germ, and tip cap are removed, leaving the endosperm. The sifted maize meal then goes through fortification process, i.e. enriched with vitamins and mineral, and is packaged and ready for distribution.

African kalo Millet Flour

The millet processing involves several stages beginning with cleaning. Cleaning process involves de-husking, de-stoning, polishing and grading of the millet grains. The process eliminates all the husks, sand, metallic objects, stones, dust, undesirable grains and any other foreign matter that could be present in the millet. The millet is milled to the desired particle size using a hammer mill. It is then sifted to guarantee uniformity in particle size of the final flour. Once quality of the flour is assured, it is machine packed, and ready for distribution.

Once the grain has passed inspection it is unloaded and stored into large bins/ silos or in the go down. Storing grain is a science. The right moisture, temperatures and air must be maintained or the grain may mould, sprout, or ferment.

During storage the grain may go through a fumigation process to eliminate insect pests. Grain is stored according to colour and other quality considerations.

Before any tuber or grain is unloaded at the facility, samples are taken to ensure it passes inspection. Potatoes and Grains are tested for moisture content, infestation, unsound tubers, kernels/seeds, foreign material and Aflatoxin.
